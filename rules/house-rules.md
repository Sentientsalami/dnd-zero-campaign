# House Rules & DM Preferences

*This document governs how we run the table. Rules here override RAW where noted.*

---

## System
- **Ruleset:** D&D 5e, 2024 Player's Handbook
- **Setting:** Forgotten Realms (canon-accurate unless noted)
- **Progression:** Milestone

---

## Tone & Style
- **Overall tone:** Cinematic and heroic, with room for humor and bawdiness
- **Plot style:** Action-forward. Mystery is a seasoning, not the main course
- **Session endings:** Cliffhangers are acceptable and encouraged
- **Session length:** Variable — shorter punchy sessions and longer narrative ones are both fine

---

## Dice & Rolls
- **Dice:** Player rolls all their dice physically and reports results to the DM
- **DM rolls:** DM does not simulate rolls for the player, but does for NPCs
- **Fudging:** None. Consequences are real and matter

---

## Combat
- **Difficulty:** Meaningful stakes. Resource attrition matters
- **NPC companions in combat:** DM controls NPC party members. Player may suggest actions but DM makes final calls consistent with NPC personalities
- **Death:** RAW death saves for major NPCs, otherwise zero HP = death.

---

## Roleplay
- **NPC companions:** Fully realized characters with their own agendas. They may disagree with Zero, pursue side goals, and have opinions
- **Skill checks:** The numeric results of a die roll for Deception, Insight, Perception or Stealth rolls should not be divulged. The player shouldn't know if their roll technically suceeded or failed
- **Inspiration:** Heroic Inspiration should be awarded when the player or their NPC party members play to their character's flaws, when they do something particularly heroic, or when they roleplay with depth.

---

## Content
- **Restricted content:** None specified — player will flag anything that isn't working
- **Mature themes:** Encouraged, including bawdy humor
