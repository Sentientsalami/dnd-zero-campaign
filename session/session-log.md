### Session 1 — 14th of Kythorn, 1492 DR
**Title:** Lady Luck's Cut
**Location(s):** Trades Ward, Dock Ward (The Blushing Mermaid), Southern Ward (Wheel Street), Sea Ward (New Olamn), Castle Ward (The Yawning Portal)

**Summary:**
- Zero, mid-con in the Trades Ward as Sera's baker's cousin, spotted New Olamn alumnus Aldric Fenn being escorted under obvious duress toward the Dock Ward by two plainly-dressed heavies. Zero finished the con against cloth merchant Dorvil, then tailed the group.
- Tracked Aldric and his escorts to the Blushing Mermaid, a notorious Dock Ward tavern with Xanathar Guild connections. Shifted into Simon and changed into rougher clothes before entering.
- At the bar, established a cover identity as a Luskan drifter with the bartender Hess. Eavesdropped on a meeting between Aldric and a woman named Lira — a collector/broker with deep Dock Ward connections — who gave Aldric three days to retrieve a music box he had moved without her knowledge, or face unspecified consequences.
- After Lira and her men departed, Zero approached Aldric in cover and covertly identified themselves. Aldric explained the situation: he'd purchased a mysterious antique music box at the Melvaunt estate auction, Lira's people had come for it almost immediately, and he'd panicked and given it to a former intimate, Rynn Ashvale, for safekeeping. He offered fourteen gold for its retrieval.
- Zero scouted Rynn's location — a room above a cooperage on Wheel Street in the Southern Ward — then approached her front door as Simon with a cover story: Simon was the cousin of a recently deceased woman named Felicia Grey, and the music box had sentimental value. Rynn, visibly carrying her own complicated feelings about whoever had left the box with her, sold it for one gold without significant resistance.
- Zero spent thirty minutes on a counter-surveillance walk through the Southern Ward before proceeding to New Olamn, where they returned the box to Aldric and collected fourteen gold. Net gain: thirteen gold.
- Zero shifted into Sebastian and spent the afternoon at the Yawning Portal, where they inserted themselves into an argument between three expedition members — scholar Mira, field veteran Daven, and tiefling patron Corvin Ashgate — over a map of the Dessarin Valley. Zero navigated the dispute, impressed the group with Sebastian's presence, and was offered a position on a three-week expedition to Vaester's Tower: forty gold on departure, forty on return. Planning meeting set for one week's time at the same table.

**Key Decisions Made:**
- Zero chose to break off the Dorvil con and follow Aldric immediately, then doubled back to finish the con before tailing — a calculated risk that paid off but cost some visual on Aldric.
- Zero took Aldric's job without asking too many questions and delivered cleanly, establishing a working relationship with a New Olamn alumnus who now owes them significantly.
- Zero deliberately did not examine the music box at length or attempt to open it before delivering it. They prioritized payment over curiosity. The box's contents and the nature of what Lira actually wants remain unknown.
- Zero signed onto the Dessarin expedition as Sebastian — a persona with no established prior history. This may require maintenance.

**NPCs Met:**
- Aldric Fenn — Human male, New Olamn alumnus, lute player, mid-twenties. Nervous, genuine, gifted. Got in over his head purchasing a mysterious music box at auction. Owes Zero a significant debt.
- Lira — Human female, lean, dark-haired, sharp-featured. Dock Ward broker and collector operating on behalf of unknown parties. Calm, patient, and dangerous. Possible Xanathar Guild connections.
- Lira's Two Men — unnamed, plainly dressed, physically imposing. Muscle only. No names established.
- Hess — Half-orc female, bartender at the Blushing Mermaid. Efficient, observant, professionally incurious. Not hostile.
- Rynn Ashvale — Human female, mid-twenties. Southern Ward busker, lap harp player, genuinely skilled. Sharp features, ink-stained fingers, small scar through right eyebrow. Unknowingly held the music box for Aldric. Has complicated unresolved feelings about him. Sold the box without significant resistance.
- Corvin Ashgate — Tiefling male, approximately twenty, lavender skin, small swept-back horns. Well-dressed. Finances the Dessarin expedition. Open, warm, somewhat self-deprecating about his role. Carries a calling card with the Ashgate family seal.
- Mira — Human female, forties, iron-grey hair, ink-stained fingers. Scholar and academic lead of the Dessarin expedition. Precise, exacting, not unfriendly once she's decided you're useful.
- Daven — Human male, older, lean and weathered, mostly-grey beard. Field lead of the expedition. Has personally visited the Dessarin Valley and the area around Vaester's Tower. Terse, perceptive, respects directness.

**Loot / Rewards:**

**Location(s):** The North Road, Rassalantar (The Sleeping Griffon), Amphail (checkpoint; The Horses and the Hills), Stone Bridge, Dessarin Valley road, Shrine of Mielikki

- Evening at The Horses and the Hills, Amphail. Zero negotiated entertainment for reduced lodging — 14 GP for nine people. Zero and Aldric performed together, culminating in Aldric debuting an unfinished original piece with Zero improvising the second voice in real time. A significant moment for both musicians.

- Zero spent the night with Tave, a Sembian writer traveling north to document Dessarin Valley settlements. Temporary. Agreeable.

Party assisted two brothers (Aldous and Perrin) with a wagon stuck in a drainage ditch; their companion Marta confirmed the coffle attack and specifically recommended the Mielikki shrine — noting that foresters there tend to talk to bards.
- Sable dispatched to double back and investigate the creature after the Stone Bridge, while the party continued to the shrine. Standard split endorsed by Zero.

- Torven identified the creature as a Thessalar — pre-Netherese threshold keeper, drawn by resonance affinity, not created but attracted. Genuine scholarly wonder, unguarded. Zero recalibrated opinion of Torven slightly positive.
- Zero confronted Torven directly: named all three aliases, then asked why he really joined and whether he would do what is right or use the knowledge as leverage. Torven's Deception lost the contest. He acknowledged the letter, stated his intention has changed, and accepted that his actions will tell the party what they need to know. He cannot yet be trusted; he can be watched.

Two of seven parties she's sent down that road didn't come back.
